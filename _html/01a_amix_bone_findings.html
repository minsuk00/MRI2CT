What 11 Experiments Measured —
and where the real novelty is

A standalone read of the empirical campaign run on your SynthRAD data. The lit-review lives in 01_sota_mri2ct_translation.html; this is just the findings + the plan.

Frozen Anatomix v1.4 ~1.7M body voxels, all 5 regions 11 experiments · multi-seed proxy scale (no full training)

Bottom line up front

Your best model is a strong-augmentation U-Net, and no single-MR loss/architecture/feature/injection trick will beat it — because the only metric with headroom (bone) is information-limited, not optimization-limited. Anatomix features, perceptual losses, and bone-localizers are all deterministic functions of the MR, so a high-capacity translator re-derives them: they add no information and cannot lower the bone error floor. The two doors that can move results: (1) add an information source (atlas / population-CT prior, or multi-contrast), or (2) change the objective (generative bone for realism/dose, where MAE is floored). That negative result is the valuable part: it rules out weeks of dead-end full trainings.

1How these numbers were produced

Everything below is a proxy: a small 3D U-Net (MONAI BasicUNet), ~160 training patches (96³) from 4 subjects/region, 1000 steps, light flip-augmentation, 2–3 seeds, held-out val subjects. The point is fast relative comparison and ruling things in/out in minutes — not final numbers. Metric is MAE in HU, broken down by tissue (air <−300, soft −300..200, bone >200), because aggregate MAE is dominated by easy soft tissue and hides the bone story. Scripts: notebooks/e6–e11*.py.

Read the magnitudes as relative

Proxy-scale absolute MAEs are higher than your full pipeline; the orderings, the frontier shape, and the oracle-vs-realistic gap are what transfer. All flagged for full-scale confirmation (§4, T0/T1).

2Part A — Why Anatomix doesn't help (E1–E6)

Confirmed good

Anatomix features are not the problem. They're anatomically rich (linear seg macro-F1 0.40 vs 0.21 for raw MR) and genuinely cross-modal (φ(MR)·φ(CT) cosine 0.93). The foundation model works as designed.

The mechanism

φ's advantage vanishes with capacity: linear readout φ wins (HU MAE 232 vs 294), but a small MLP ties it (138 vs 133). A high-capacity translator re-derives φ-equivalent features from the raw MR by itself.

→ feeding φ in is redundant, which is why concat-17ch doesn't beat plain MR at scale.

Law

φ helps only when the translator is too small/data-starved to re-derive it. Tiny model: MR+φ = 165.6 vs MR-only = 191.8 HU (−14%). This is also why "strong aug → U-Net wins, weak aug → Anatomix wins": augmentation and φ are substitutes for the same robustness.

3Part B — The bone bottleneck (E7–E11)

You asked for a method that makes results better. Augmentation already supplies the robustness φ offered, so I targeted the one place with headroom it can't touch: bone (MAE 3–5× soft; the metric that matters for dose/planning). Eleven experiments later, the answer is decisive.

A realistic bone-localizer trained on MR/φ captures almost none of the oracle gain — because predicting bone location from MR hits the same information wall, and the translator already does it internally. The oracle's 53% came from ground-truth location, i.e. information not in single-contrast MR.

The unified law (ties Part A + Part B together)

The bone floor is set by what the MR physically contains about bone — period. Anything that is a deterministic function of the MR (Anatomix φ, perceptual losses, bone-localizers, frequency/edge losses) is re-derived by a high-capacity, well-augmented translator. None add information; none lower the floor. φ-redundancy (Part A) and the bone-ceiling (Part B) are the same phenomenon.

4Where the real novelty is

Two doors remain — both add what the MR lacks or change what you optimize. Plus the diagnostic itself.

1Atlas / population-prior injection — add information

The oracle says bone-location is worth −53% and E11 says it isn't in the MR. A registered cohort/atlas CT (or a φ-retrieved nearest training CT) injects that distributional bone information — the one input that's not a function of the MR, so the translator can't re-derive it. This is "inject something," done right. Highest chance of a real bone win. Needs inter-subject registration.

2Generative bone-residual — change the objective

L1 regresses bone to the mean → blur. A diffusion/GAN refiner on the bone residual (conditioned on your regression sCT) matches the bone HU distribution → sharp, realistic cortices and better Dice/texture/dose, even where mean MAE is floored. Reframes the metric from MAE (bounded) to realism/downstream (achievable).

3The information-ceiling diagnostic — the general insight

The localization-vs-magnitude decomposition (oracle = −53%, recoverable as classification at AUC 0.97 but unusable because redundant with MR) is a sharp, novel result that explains why a decade of auxiliary-feature / perceptual / cascade sCT methods give only marginal bone gains. Strong ICLR/NeurIPS framing; pairs as the motivation half of a paper whose method half is #1 or #2.

Don't bother (ruled out empirically)

φ as input/loss · bone-weighting / GDL / FFL / classification / multitask / gated heads · localize-then-translate from MR or φ. All slide along the frontier or are redundant — none beat a strong-aug U-Net.
