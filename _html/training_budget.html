Training sample budget — MRI2CT baselines

Goal: keep the number of training samples (patch / slab / volume) roughly matched across models — no foreground-voxel normalization, just raw sample counts.  •  numbers are the actual info/samples_seen pulled from each model's wandb run, not config defaults.

TL;DR — reference = amix / unet ≈ 3.2M patches (128³) The real reference is 3.20M 128³ patches (800 epochs × 500 steps × batch 8), not the 4.0M the current train.py default (1000 epochs) would give. Against that: MC-DDPM over-trained at 12.0M tiny 128×128×4 slabs (and was still cut off at ep 3004/7000); MAISI (0.60M) and cWDM (0.32M, run failed well short of its 1.2M target) under-trained in full-volume units. KoalAI is one model per region at 0.50M patches each — already in the same ballpark as amix/unet's per-region share of 3.2M (0.56–1.11×). Caveat: a slab ≠ a patch ≠ a volume, so raw counts across different units are not 1:1 — that asymmetry is the price of keeping this simple.

2. KoalAI — per-region share of the 3.2M reference

amix/unet train one model over all 427 training subjects, so each region only gets a slice of the 3.2M budget — proportional to how many of those subjects belong to it. KoalAI trains a separate model per region, so the fair comparison is KoalAI's 0.5M against that per-region share, not against the full 3.2M.

KoalAI at 0.5M patches/region already sits inside 0.56–1.11× of the per-region share — close enough that no change is needed for a "similar samples" claim. To match the share exactly, bump KoalAI epochs from 1000 to share / (250 × 2) ≈ 1280 (avg) or 1364 (thorax / head-neck). Pelvis is the only region where amix/unet meaningfully out-sample KoalAI (1.8×).

Caveat (deliberately not normalized). This report counts samples, not voxels. A 128×128×4 MC-DDPM slab holds 1/32 the voxels of a 128³ amix patch, and a full MAISI/cWDM volume holds several patches' worth. Matching raw sample counts across these units is the simple approximation you asked for — it does not equalize gradient signal or compute. If a reviewer pushes on fairness, the honest line is "matched on sample count, units differ," not "matched on data seen."
